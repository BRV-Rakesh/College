Sign In

If you are using Gmail, Google’s free and hugely popular webmail client (more than 1.5 billion users, accounting for 20% of the world’s email traffic), then you probably only look at your email login history when you are informed of suspicious activity on your account. Any time you use a new device (like a new smartphone or tablet, or a shared computer somewhere) to log into your Gmail account, Google sends an alert to the device or account you’ve specified for such security notifications.

In order to detect unauthorized users, you need to know who the authorized users are. More specifically, Gmail doesn’t really track users, it tracks sessions, and those sessions are identified by the device, the browser software, and the IP address used for access. Accordingly, you will need to know which devices you’ve used to access your Gmail account, the IP addresses from which you’ve accessed it, the browsers you used to do it, and the exact dates when you logged on to check your email.
